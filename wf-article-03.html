Most commercial real estate prospecting starts with a spreadsheet and a question: “who might buy this building?” The broker pulls a list from CoStar or LoopNet, adds some names from their network, and starts calling. The list is one-dimensional — it contains one type of buyer, usually investors, because that is who the platform was designed to find. The Targeting Engine starts differently. It starts with coordinates and a property config, and it generates seven completely different prospect lists simultaneously — each calibrated to a different buyer motivation, each enriched with Clay data, each ready for personalized outreach.

Targeting AgentCONF 0.97

The difference is not just volume. It is coverage. A 48,000 SF industrial building in Kansas City’s Northland submarket is relevant to an investor seeking 7% cap rates, a logistics company seeking distribution space, a developer seeking redevelopment potential, an adjacent property owner seeking expansion, a 1031 exchange buyer on a 180-day clock, a tenant-rep with a client seeking that exact square footage, and a contract manufacturer seeking heavy-power industrial space. Each of these buyers has a different motivation, a different timeline, and a different objection set. The Targeting Engine produces a separate list for each one.

01The 7 Prospect List Types

Each list type is generated by a different search strategy. The Geographic Specialist handles adjacent owners and nearby businesses. The Transaction Specialist scans recent sales and exchanges. The Industry Specialist matches property use cases to operator types. The Network Specialist identifies tenant-rep relationships and broker activity.

02Clay Integration: From Names to Profiles

The raw prospect lists are names and addresses. They become qualified prospect profiles through Clay enrichment. Clay adds company size, revenue range, recent transactions, technology stack, funding status, LinkedIn profiles of decision-makers, and verified contact information. The enrichment rate is 94% — meaning 94% of prospects receive full profile data before entering the Outreach Agent’s email sequences.

This enrichment transforms the Outreach Agent’s capabilities. Without Clay, the email says “Dear property owner.” With Clay, it says “Your portfolio of 12 industrial properties in the Kansas City metro, including the 65,000 SF building you acquired in 2024 at $72/SF, suggests you’re actively building industrial exposure in this submarket.” The second email gets opened. The first one doesn’t.

“Without Clay enrichment, the email says ‘Dear property owner.’ With it, the email references their specific portfolio, recent acquisitions, and investment thesis. That is the difference between 18% and 34% open rates.”

03LinkedIn Filters: Professional Context at Scale

The Network Specialist coordinates with the LinkedIn Agent to apply list-type-specific filters. Investor prospects are filtered by “real estate” in title or company description plus recent posts about acquisitions. Developer prospects are filtered by “development” or “construction” in company type. Tenant-rep prospects are filtered by “broker” or “advisor” in title plus the specific property type in recent activity.

The LinkedIn profiles are cross-referenced with Clay data to build composite prospect profiles with professional context, company data, and contact information. This composite profile is what the Outreach Agent reads when it generates personalized email sequences.

Targeting AgentCONF 0.95

04Property Type Calibration

The Targeting Engine reads the property type from the shared config and adjusts all 7 list generation strategies automatically. An industrial property generates logistics operators, manufacturers, and distribution companies in the Industry-Specific list. A retail property generates restaurant groups, franchisees, and specialty retailers. A medical office building generates medical groups, dental practices, and outpatient surgery centers.

The 1031 Exchange list filters for investors who recently sold the same property type. The Adjacent Owner list filters by complementary zoning. The calibration is automatic — it reads from the config, not from manual input. This means the Targeting Engine produces relevant lists for any property type without configuration changes.

Targeting AgentCONF 0.96

0590 Seconds: Speed as a Competitive Advantage

The 4 specialist agents run in parallel. The Geographic Specialist identifies adjacent owners and nearby businesses simultaneously with the Transaction Specialist scanning recent sales and exchanges. The Industry Specialist matches property use cases to operator types while the Network Specialist identifies tenant-rep relationships. Clay enrichment adds approximately 30 seconds for a typical list size.

Total time from coordinates to enriched prospect lists: approximately 2 minutes. In a traditional CRE operation, building one prospect list for one property type takes 2–3 hours. The Targeting Engine produces seven lists in the time it takes to get coffee. The speed advantage compounds across 78 properties — the system can regenerate every prospect list for the entire portfolio in under 3 hours.
