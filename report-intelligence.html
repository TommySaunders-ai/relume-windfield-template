Executive Summary

Seven-year trajectory and competitive advantage in one view.

Windfield Real Estate has grown from a ~$10M/year brokerage in 2020 to a $30M+ annual volume operation by 2025, generating over $650K in annual commissions. The firm's competitive advantage rests on three structural pillars: deep relationships with a concentrated group of repeat owner-operators in the Kansas City Northland, a dual role as both principal (property owner) and broker that captures equity and commission on Danner-interest deals, and dominance in vacant ground sales for commercial pad sites.

This report identifies the top relationships, decodes the patterns behind the most profitable deals, and maps where prospecting effort pays the highest return.

"Fewer clients. Deeper work. Every parcel on the corridor, every owner, every zoning issue."

The Windfield Playbook

How the money gets made -- Patterns 1 & 2.

Pattern 01
The Subdivide-and-Sell
Highest commission yield
Danner entity or client acquires a large tract of raw land.
Windfield lists individual pad sites for sale.
National tenants or developers buy pads at retail pricing.
Windfield earns commission on each pad sale -- often on both sides.
Examples
Barry Plaza lots (9C, Lot 2, Lot 5) -- Creekside lots (Heaterz, Christian Brothers, Costa Oil) -- Staley Corners (Casey's, O'Reilly, Sig Acquisition) -- GH14 lots (McDonald's, CKC, N3) -- Fairview lots -- Twin Creeks lots -- Richardson Plaza lots.
Pattern 02
The Relationship Recycler
Buy -- lease -- sell
Help client buy a property (buyer rep, 3%).
Manage and lease the property for them (landlord rep, 6% of lease value).
Eventually help them sell it (seller rep, 3-6%).
Examples
Shane Crees / KCPI: bought 1501 S 169 ($2M) -- Windfield leased tenants -- sold Diversified Building ($3.125M). Shane Crees: bought Buckners ($1.5M) -- Windfield leased 48 Hour Auto Hail -- listed for resale ($1.85M) -- sold to Domsons ($1.65M). CKC Holdings: bought GH14 Lot 4 ($3.059M) -- built Kelly Crossing -- Windfield leases tenants -- sold Hwy N Storage ($2.1M).

The Windfield Playbook

Patterns 3 & 4 -- the principal and the anchor.

Pattern 03
The Personal Interest Multiplier
Equity + commission
Danner entity acquires property (earns buyer commission).
Windfield leases tenants (per-lease commission).
Danner entity sells property (sale commission + captured equity gain).
Examples
Split Oak Mall: bought -- leased 8+ tenants -- sold for $800K. 4031 NW Barry Rd: Sherwin Williams $52K lease commission -- building sold for $2.24M. 8331 N Green Hills: $299K commission on $8.3M sale. 1200 Burlington: bought, leasing ongoing. Kelly Crossing: bought $2.15M, leasing Montessori and Misers.
Pattern 04
The Anchor Tenant Play
National tenants pay premium
List vacant ground for commercial development.
Attract a national tenant (DQ, Casey's, Christian Brothers, Waffle House, O'Reilly, Camp Bow Wow).
Close the sale once the anchor commits.
National tenants pay premium for shovel-ready pad sites.
