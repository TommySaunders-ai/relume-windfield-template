About the Book

From surviving to thriving

Beyond Surviving is more than a self-help book — it's a clinical roadmap for anyone who has ever felt trapped by their own emotions. Written by Edward Flynn, a licensed therapist with over 13 years of experience treating anxiety, trauma, and emotional dysregulation, this book translates the full scope of Dialectical Behavior Therapy into accessible, actionable practice.

Across 15 chapters and 4 core DBT modules, you'll learn the same skills taught in clinical DBT programs — distress tolerance, emotion regulation, interpersonal effectiveness, and mindfulness — along with Edward's unique integration of clinical hypnotherapy techniques that deepen the work and accelerate results.

Each chapter includes guided exercises, real client scenarios (anonymized), reflection prompts, and practical worksheets. Whether you're a therapy client looking for supplemental material, a clinician seeking a client-friendly resource, or someone navigating emotional challenges on your own, this book meets you where you are and walks with you toward a life that feels genuinely worth living.

About the Author

Edward Flynn

LPC, NBCCH, DBTC — Licensed Professional Counselor

Edward Flynn is a Licensed Professional Counselor, Nationally Board Certified Clinical Hypnotherapist, and certified DBT clinician based in Pennsylvania. With over 13 years of clinical experience, he specializes in treating anxiety disorders, trauma, depression, and emotional dysregulation using a unique integration of evidence-based DBT and clinical hypnotherapy.

Edward's approach combines the structured skill-building of DBT with the deep, experiential change work of hypnotherapy — helping clients not just manage symptoms, but fundamentally transform their relationship with their emotions. He has trained hundreds of therapists and helped thousands of clients build lives they genuinely want to live.
