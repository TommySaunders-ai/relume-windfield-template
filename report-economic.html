Strategic Framing

Not a product. An operating system.

"Windfield IO is not an AI product. It is a proprietary operating system that lets Windfield execute commercial real estate at a scale, speed, and intelligence density no KC competitor can match."
The operating thesis
The Performance-Marketing Framing
Every action measured. Every dollar accounted.
Each agent-driven action has a measured cost (~$7.71), a measured conversion funnel (0.3% action → closed deal), and a measured revenue (~$38.5K GCI per deal). Projected CAC of ~$2,570 is 6-15× below the industry baseline of $15-40K per closed deal.
The Competitive Positioning
A 12-24 month uncontested lead.
91% of KC CRE firms score below 8/25 on Digital Maturity. Only 10 firms are real digital threats. Not one has MCP endpoints, AEO-ready schema, or agentic loops. Windfield's Dual-Mode MCP approach is uncontested in this market.
The Economic Asymmetry
$286K builds it. $22.7M comes back.
Year 1 build of ~$286K. Year 2+ recurring ~$565K/yr. Replaces the productive equivalent of 12-25 FTEs at $1.08M-$2.25M/yr. Base 3Y outcome: +~$22.7M net contribution. Ambitious: +~$51.5M. Plus portfolio acquisition optionality on every deal sourced.
The Operating Discipline
Build one loop. Measure against CAC. Kill what fails.
Build one loop at a time, measure every loop against its own CAC and LTV, kill what doesn't work, compound what does. Instrument the four-layer KPI pyramid from day one. Year 1 is experimentation. Year 2 is where compounding kicks in.

Why 22× matters
The 1.5× repeat-client multiplier baked into the LTV is the most important assumption in the model. If we swap the 12-month LTV for the observed 3-year LTV, the ratio exceeds 100×.

Gate Conditions & Investment Required

What Year 1 must prove to unlock Year 2.

The commitment isn't $286K → $22.7M. The commitment is $286K to prove Year 1 works — then decide whether to invest the Year 2 recurring. Explicit gates below.

Scale Triggers · Green
If these hit by end of Year 1, commit Year 2 recurring.
  • Measured action → deal rate ≥ 0.25% across 4+ loops
  • At least 5 closed deals sourced via system by M12
  • Measured CAC ≤ $4,000 (generous band on $2,570 projection)
  • MCP endpoints serving ≥50 inbound AI queries/month
  • At least one second-deal repeat from a system-sourced client
Hold & Tune · Yellow
If these are the reality, extend Year 1 by 6 months before committing.
  • Action → deal rate in 0.15-0.25% band
  • 2-4 closed deals sourced — evidence but not velocity
  • Measured CAC in $4K-$8K band — still 3× better than industry
  • MCP uptime <95% or high error rates in sourcing loops
  • Voice AI approval gate too slow — tune down the broker review time first
Kill Conditions · Red
If these are the reality, shut it down — don't escalate.
  • Action → deal rate < 0.1% across all loops
  • Zero closed deals sourced by M12
  • Measured CAC > $10,000 with no clear path to compression
  • Broker approval time trending up, not down (model diverging)
  • No inbound MCP queries — external AI ecosystem ignoring the endpoints

Methodology & Model Structure

How this computes.

The Economic Impact Model is a live Excel model across seven sheets. Assumptions holds all editable inputs (blue cells). Unit Economics computes the $7.71 cost-per-action and derives CAC. Scenarios projects 36 months of monthly volume, conversions, deals, GCI, and net contribution across Conservative / Base / Ambitious scenarios — every cell a formula. Cost Structure details Year 1 build and Year 2+ recurring with Low / Base / High sensitivity bands. KPI Dashboard is the four-layer measurement template for replacing projections with actuals.

This is not a forecast. It is a decision support framework. The projections should not be committed to externally or used as customer-facing claims. Their job is to size the Year 1 investment, calibrate the gate conditions, and provide a baseline against which real instrumented data can be compared once the system is running.

Pair this model with the Relationship Intelligence Report (RPT_windfield_relationship_intelligence_2026-04-22.html) — that report establishes the historical transaction base that this model's assumptions derive from: blended $38.5K GCI per deal, 65% repeat-client rate, Tier 1 depth that supports the 1.5× multiplier. The two documents are intended to be read as a pair: the first proves the insight value, this one prices the investment required to operationalize it.
